— FAQ —

Q&A on the back of a napkin.

Is the software really free?

Yes. The whole app is open-source MIT and runs from Walrus Sites. We charge for Notebook + Archive because teams want hosted dashboards, branded domains, and someone to call. Solo hackers pay nothing forever.

Why is Walrus 100× cheaper?

One submission can have many fields and attachments. Naive blob storage = one Walrus write per file. Quilt lets us batch the whole submission into a single atomic write — gas + storage cost drops accordingly.

What happens after 12 epochs?

By default we set a 12-epoch (~6 month) lifetime on stored blobs. Before they expire we’ll prompt you to extend, archive, or let them lapse. Sealed metadata stays on Sui forever — only the body is on Walrus.

Can I migrate off?

Always. Your data is just Walrus blobs + Sui state. We have a one-command export tool that hands you the raw schema, blobs, and decrypted submissions in JSON. No lock-in.

Do you accept fiat?

For Notebook + Archive, yes (Stripe). For on-chain costs, no — you’ll need a Sui wallet with some SUI in it. Use the in-app on-ramp.

What about non-profits?

Email hi@catat.io. Notebook is free for verified non-profits, public good projects, and student hackathons.
