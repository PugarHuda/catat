— what makes catat different —

Form software, finally honest about its data.

Tally and Typeform ask you to trust their dashboards. catat hands you the receipts: every submission has a Sui tx hash, every byte lives in Walrus, every secret is sealed with a 2-of-3 key.

01 Privacy · Seal

Per-field encryption with Seal.

Toggle 🔒 on any field. Email and screenshots stay sealed end-to-end with threshold encryption — only the form owner’s wallet can decrypt, in the browser, never on a server.

id = formId · fieldId  — granular like nothing else.
02 Provable

On-chain proof, every time.

Every submission emits a Sui event & registers a blob ID. Your “500 responses” is a fact, not a claim.

03 Storage · Quilt

Walrus storage, Quilt-batched.

One submission + all attachments → one Quilt → one write. ~100× cheaper than naive blob-per-file.

12 epochs default · extendable
04 Gating

Token-gated by default.

token_gate = 0x… — only NFT or coin holders can submit. No off-chain allowlist plumbing.

05 Self-host · OSS

Hosted on Walrus Sites — yours forever.

The whole app is a decentralized site. Fork the repo, point at your testnet package ID, deploy. No backend, no database, no monthly bill — just Sui state + Walrus bytes + Seal keys.

MIT licensed · 100% open source
